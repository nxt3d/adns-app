This page is a footnote, not the headline.

aDNS names are **their own class of name**. The product is the bare aDNS form —
`normies.yoel`. You do not need to know anything below to use aDNS.

## What happens under the hood

For compatibility with existing resolvers and tooling, aDNS names resolve through an
ENS-compatible namespace rooted at `adns.eth`. The public aDNS form maps to a conventional
out-to-in name:

```text
aDNS form:     normies.yoel
under the hood: yoel.normies.adns.eth
```

- The apex is `adns.eth`.
- The first level (`normies`) is the collection slug / parent namespace.
- The second level (`yoel`) is the name you registered.

## Why this is only a footnote

- **It is not a marketing claim.** aDNS is not “an ENS subdomain” — it is a names protocol
  for NFTs that happens to resolve through an ENS-compatible apex.
- **You never type it.** The registration widget and resolvers handle the mapping. Users
  read and share `normies.yoel`.
- **It exists for interop.** Wallets and resolvers that already understand ENS-style names
  can resolve aDNS names without special-casing, because the underlying name is a
  conventional hierarchical name.

<Callout type="info">
  If you are building a resolver-level integration, the `.adns.eth` apex matters. For
  everything else — registering, displaying, and sharing names — the aDNS form is all you
  need.
</Callout>
